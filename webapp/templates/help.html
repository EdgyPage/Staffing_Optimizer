How this works

You describe departments and how work flows between them; the app computes the steady-state staffing — how many people each department needs so nothing backs up.

The workflow

  1. Build a network in the Builder: add departments, then drag node → node (or right-drag) to link them.
  2. Give each department a makespan; set the demand entering the root and the headcount.
  3. Validate (the builder flags problems live and shows a status per department), then Save.
  4. Analyze to read the steady-state staffing, or Simulate to watch backlog over time.

The rules

  • Makespan — time to process one unit, per person (e.g. minutes/unit). Must be > 0.
  • Transfer ratio — the fraction (or %) of a department's output sent to another. The ratios leaving a department can sum to under 100% (the rest exits the system) or over 100% (fan-out / work multiplication).
  • Root & demand — a root has no inbound flow; that's where outside demand enters (e.g. inbound trucks). Work has to enter somewhere.
  • Loops must drain — rework is fine (e.g. 90% forward, 40% back) as long as work eventually leaves. A loop that never drains (e.g. 100% each way) is rejected as unstable.
  • Headcount (S) and time per employee (T) — the closed pool of people, and the productive time each supplies per period (e.g. 480 min/shift).
  • Congestion β and buffer (optional) — used only by the time-step Simulation: backlog makes a department slower (β), and a full buffer pushes back on its upstream.

The two answers (Analyze)

  • Steady-state staffing — given makespans, transfer ratios and demand, the required people per department and the split %, plus whether it fits your headcount.
  • Capacity at a given staffing — for a staffing level you choose, each department's throughput and which one is the bottleneck.
